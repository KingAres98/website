// 01 — ABOUT

Who I Am

I'm a graduate student and early-career researcher working at the intersection of cybersecurity, embedded systems, and autonomous technology. My work centers on making the software systems that power our world — from connected vehicles to critical infrastructure — harder to compromise and easier to trust.

Before pivoting fully into research, I spent two years in IT Governance, Risk & Compliance, where I learned how organizations actually think about risk — and how wide the gap is between policy and real technical security. That experience sharpened my perspective: good security research has to live in the real world.

Off the clock, I'm wrenching on a Chevy Camaro ZL1 build, creating content about cars and traveling on TikTok, gaming, and exploring whatever new hobby catches my attention. I take the same obsessive energy I bring to research and apply it everywhere else.

// 03 — RESEARCH & INTERESTS

Technical Focus

My research sits at the edge of systems security — where cryptography meets constrained devices, and where a single compromised update can ground a fleet or disable a vehicle. Here's what I'm working on and thinking about.

⬡ Primary Focus

Secure Software Updates & Uptane

My primary research focus is designing a secure communication protocol for delivering software updates to fleet UAV systems. This means tackling real-world challenges: authenticated channels, integrity verification, and ensuring updates reach the right device even in contested or degraded environments — without compromising the fleet.

Uptane OTA Updates Supply Chain
⬡

UAV & Drone Security

Unmanned aerial systems are a rapidly expanding attack surface. My interest is in securing the full UAV stack — command-and-control links, firmware delivery, and inter-drone communication in swarm scenarios. The goal is resilient, authenticated systems that hold up in real operational conditions, not just lab environments.

UAV RF Security Firmware
⬡

Ethical Hacking & Penetration Testing

Understanding how systems break is fundamental to building systems that don't. I'm actively developing skills in penetration testing and ethical hacking — network enumeration, vulnerability assessment, exploitation techniques, and responsible disclosure. Security research and offensive skills go hand in hand.

Pentesting Enumeration Vuln Assessment
⬡

Emerging Tech & Future Vectors

The attack surface of tomorrow is being built today. I follow developments in AI-assisted exploitation, quantum-resistant cryptography, autonomous systems policy, and the security implications of 5G/6G connectivity for embedded fleets.

Secure Comms Post-Quantum AI Security 5G/6G
⬡ Upcoming

V2X & Connected Vehicle Security

Vehicle-to-Everything communication is one of the most security-critical frontiers in emerging tech. I'm preparing to enter lab research on V2X — exploring how vehicles authenticate with infrastructure, other vehicles, and networks, and where those trust models break down under adversarial conditions.

V2X Connected Vehicles Protocol Security
